# 1. Introduction

The modern aviation industry operates within a highly sophisticated and data-intensive environment, requiring the seamless coordination of flight schedules, passenger logistics, aircraft maintenance, and personnel deployment. However, existing airline management frameworks often rely on fragmented legacy systems or manual workflows that create significant operational bottlenecks, such as data silos, information redundancy, and a high risk of human error during booking and scheduling processes. These limitations result in a lack of real-time visibility across departments, leading to synchronization delays and inconsistent data management that ultimately compromise organizational efficiency and the overall passenger experience.

To address these systemic challenges, a centralized and automated digital platform is essential to modernize traditional airline operations through scalable, high-performance architecture. The proposed system, "Airline Management System," is designed to address these challenges by providing a centralized web-based platform that automates the core workflows of airline operations. Developed using a robust technological stack consisting of a React-based interactive frontend and a Flask-driven RESTful backend, this system implements a layered architecture to separate presentation, business logic, and data management. By providing role-based access control and integrated modules for flight scheduling, fleet tracking, and passenger booking, the system effectively optimizes resource allocation and ensures a seamless data flow across all operational units, thereby enhancing the overall reliability of the airline management lifecycle.
